Design Rules

This page is intended to fulfill the requirements of the "Build a Website Design System" Codecademy project in the full stack development course program. For simplicity and consistency, the styling of this page was taken from a prior Codecademy project with the intention of linking them together to form a larger website. Select "Home" in the site's header navigation to view the prior project.

The design rules of my projects aim to be simple, intuitive, and accessibility focused. They also aim to utilize open source resources from trustworthy sources. This means Google Fonts and other closed source resources are not to be used. The digital world is meant to be an open environment to exchange information and ideas. Closed source resources halt that free exchange and will not be supported across my projects.

My design rules also aim to keep the user experience of a diverse audience in mind. Relevant information should be easy to locate both within a page and the site as a whole. A well designed site should allow an end user to feel they are able to quickly "get in and out" with the information they need. End users should not feel they have to spend countless hours digging amongst irrelevant information, poorly linked pages, or erroneous images and styles. They should also not feel the need to extensively modify their browser via settings, add-ons, and extensions.
